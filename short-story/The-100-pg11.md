# Page 11

Dex’s shoulder wound worsened overnight, edges swollen and hot despite antibiotics. Iris worked by lantern through intermittent rain, debriding tissue while Dex bit down on leather and refused to make noise that would scare younger camp members. Outside the infirmary, strategy arguments intensified around the newly recovered bunker data.

Shelter maps suggested three viable sites within twelve days’ travel, each with different risks: one in mountain tunnels vulnerable to collapse, one in river delta marshes with unknown inhabitants, and one under an old research complex near Ash Wolf range. Jonah favored fortifying current ground and avoiding overextension. Rhea argued static defense invited eventual siege. Talan added a river proverb: water that stops moving becomes rot.

Compromise emerged by midnight. They would dispatch three reconnaissance teams with shared flare codes, strict return deadlines, and authority limited to intelligence gathering unless directly attacked. Iris would remain at base to decode deeper files and maintain communications.

Before dawn, Dex, feverish but lucid, grabbed Rhea’s wrist and whispered that Krell was not acting independently. During the bunker fight he had heard Helios command terms in a sniper’s radio chatter. Someone in orbit was arming local factions to keep the hundred fragmented.

Rhea did not sleep after that. At first light she convened emergency council and disclosed the suspicion as unverified intelligence. Instead of panic, she got grim focus. If orbit politics had reached the ground, then local survival was now part of a larger war over legitimacy, resources, and who would define justice on a reclaimed planet.
