# Page 12

Bo Lin’s mast finally captured a clean transmission at midday, narrow-band and encrypted with maintenance protocols Iris recognized from station repair manuals. She cracked it in under an hour. The voice belonged to Councillor Orin Vale.

Vale spoke to an unidentified ground asset about “stabilization through managed conflict.” He confirmed delivery schedules for rifles, gas canisters, and crowd-control drones to “local enforcement partners,” terminology that matched Ash Wolf movements almost perfectly. In short, Helios leadership had outsourced violence.

Iris played the recording at full assembly. No one interrupted. When it ended, even Jonah, defender of structure, slammed his fist into the table and called Vale a traitor.

They revised mission priorities immediately. Holding the valley mattered, but exposing truth mattered more. If other settlements and Helios citizens heard proof, Vale’s coalition would fracture. The best broadcast point was Summit Spire, a high-altitude weather tower north of Wolf territory that could relay on emergency frequencies still recognized in orbit.

Risks were severe: mountain weather, hostile terrain, and likely interception. Still, the vote was overwhelming. Rhea would lead the summit team with Jonah, Iris, Talan, Lenn, and fourteen volunteers. Dex, still recovering, protested being sidelined until Iris threatened to sedate him.

That night they prepared relay kits, duplicate data drives, and a recorded testimony list including names of the dead from both sides. Rhea insisted on that detail. “If we tell truth selectively,” she said, “we become the same machine that exiled us.” By dawn, packs were loaded and valley sentries lit departure lanterns in silent, stubborn hope.
