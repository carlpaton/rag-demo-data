# Page 14

After the canyon fight, the survivors stood among spent casings and drifting dust, stunned by what necessity had forged. Nine from the hundred were dead. Twelve Wolves had fallen. Helios snipers lay where they had expected anonymity to protect them. No speeches could make that cost acceptable, but the bloodshed stripped illusions clean.

Rhea ordered immediate casualty triage while Jonah and Krell coordinated perimeter sweeps for remaining shooters. Talan collected insignia patches as evidence. Iris documented everything with timestamped logs, already planning how to present proof without room for denial.

Krell requested private terms. Instead, Rhea insisted on open negotiation in front of both groups. Conditions were blunt: temporary alliance to reach Summit Spire, mutual nonaggression until broadcast completed, shared medical treatment, and prisoner exchange for anyone captured alive. Krell agreed after a long silence and one hard nod.

The combined column moved before dawn, Wolves carrying half the relay equipment while the hundred shared antibiotics. Trust did not bloom; it was rationed, monitored, and reinforced by immediate consequences for violations. Strangely, that made it stronger than sentiment.

At midday they reached the lower slopes of the tower basin. Ice slicked ladder rungs and wind howled through broken relay dishes. Iris estimated four hours to restore uplink if power conduits remained intact.

As they made camp in the tower shadow, Rhea stood watch beside Krell. Neither pretended friendship. They traded inventories, casualty names, and route contingencies like accountants of war. By nightfall, both understood the same equation: if the broadcast failed, Vale would erase witnesses and rewrite history. If it succeeded, every faction would be forced to choose sides in daylight.
