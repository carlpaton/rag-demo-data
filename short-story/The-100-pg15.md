# Page 15

Summit Spire rose like a spear through cloud, its exterior ladders crusted with ice and old lightning scars. Inside, frozen corridors rattled with wind. Ancient server cabinets still hummed on backup reserves, a miracle of pre-fall engineering and stubborn maintenance cycles that had never fully died.

Iris and Sera, Bo Lin’s apprentice, rewired the uplink core using scavenged converters, copper braid, and a prayer to sane voltage. Jonah and Krell split perimeter sectors and synchronized signal whistles so mixed teams could respond without language confusion. Talan posted archers in thermal blind spots where drones might approach unseen.

At 09:17 local, power stabilized enough for transmission. Rhea recorded the message in one take: names of the dead from every side, evidence of Vale’s covert arming, and a declaration that Earth settlements would govern under public charter rather than orbital decree. She ended with a single demand: open hearings broadcast to all channels.

Iris pushed the burst through emergency frequencies tied to old civil defense networks. For two seconds, silence. Then pings returned from three unknown shelters, two civilian Helios channels, and one encrypted node flagged as internal security oversight.

The truth had escaped containment.

Celebration lasted less than a minute. Long-range sensors detected outbound craft signatures from orbit, likely retaliation or forced “peacekeeping.” They packed fast and began descent under sleet, carrying duplicate drives in separate packs in case of ambush.

By the time they reached lower timberline, response chatter from Helios was already fragmenting: denials, emergency motions, and worker unions demanding accountability. Information warfare had begun, and this time the ground had a voice loud enough to matter.
