# Page 16

Retaliation reached the valley at dusk. Two Helios gun drones descended through cloud, broadcasting surrender terms in Vale’s clipped voice and marking heat signatures for selective strikes. Panic surged, but charter drills held.

Iris triggered Lenn’s flare-grid protocol, flooding perimeter sectors with decoy thermal signatures. Jonah’s teams deployed mirrored panels to blind optical sensors while River Clan archers launched cable-tipped bolts at rotor housings. The first drone clipped a barricade and spun into the palisade in sparks.

The second adjusted quickly and strafed the infirmary zone. Canvas ignited. Three patients died before fire lines contained it. Dex, still weak but upright, climbed a watchtower with a recovered launcher from bunker stock and waited for lock tone through static. One shot. Direct hit. The drone broke apart over the riverbank.

Silence after impact felt heavier than the battle.

Rhea coordinated evacuation counts, then walked the burned infirmary with Iris in ash up to their ankles. Victory did not erase loss; it only prevented larger loss. At emergency assembly, she refused triumph language and read the names of the dead first.

New defenses were approved overnight: dispersed med tents, hardened shelters, and rotating anti-drone crews with strict ammunition controls. Krell’s fighters, honoring summit terms, stayed to help rebuild rather than withdraw.

Near midnight, Helios channels erupted with reports of labor walkouts and formal charges against Vale. The broadcast had opened fractures no drone strike could seal. Still, Rhea knew power rarely surrendered cleanly. They had survived one backlash. Another would come, probably subtler, likely political, and just as dangerous if they mistook process for justice.
