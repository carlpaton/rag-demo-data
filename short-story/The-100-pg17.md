In orbit, Helios split along lines that had been hidden by fear. Medics leaked ration fraud records. Dock workers blocked munitions transfers. Junior officers refused orders lacking civilian oversight. Emergency hearings convened under public pressure, and Vale’s bloc began to shrink in real time.

On the ground, Rhea called a great council at river bend, inviting River Clan, Ash Wolves, independent settlement scouts, and Helios delegates willing to attend unarmed. Old enemies sat in a single ring beneath rain canopies, hands close to weapons, children watching from behind supply crates.

Iris read the charter aloud in three languages. Jonah proposed a defense pact with clear limits: no raids on food stores or med zones, shared early-warning beacons, and mixed tribunals for cross-faction disputes. Talan demanded environmental clauses protecting spawning grounds and forest corridors. Krell surprised many by endorsing the pact first. “Vale profits when we bleed,” he said. “Let him choke on peace.”

The pact passed by voice, drumbeat, and signed marks.

Implementation started immediately. Joint patrols mapped neutral routes. Shared clinics treated whoever arrived first, faction labels ignored at intake. Data boards posted inventory and casualties publicly every evening. Accountability became routine, not rhetoric.

Not everyone accepted the shift. Two splinter raider cells rejected the pact and tested road teams twice in one week. Both incidents ended with arrests, not executions, under the new tribunal rules. Imperfect, slow, often frustrating, but different.

By week’s end, river traffic resumed and night fires burned without perimeter sirens. For the first time, the valley sounded less like a battlefield pause and more like a society learning the difficult mechanics of trust.
