# Page 18

Three weeks after the summit broadcast, a final shuttle descended under white signal lights and strict approach corridors. Perimeter teams from all factions surrounded the landing zone while Iris monitored transponder integrity for hidden drone payloads. None appeared.

The delegation was led not by Vale but by Acting Chancellor Lysa Renn, installed after emergency hearings suspended half the old council. With her came engineers, agronomists, trauma doctors, and seed vault inventories. Renn requested negotiations, not compliance.

Rhea refused private talks. Every term would be debated in open assembly with translators and public records. The discussions lasted two days and ran hot. River Clan challenged historical land claims. Wolves demanded amnesty terms tied to disarmament timelines. Helios technicians asked for protected work zones and guaranteed oxygen exchange shipments.

The final document, named the Valley Accord, established rotating representation by population and territory, transparent ration systems, independent audit teams, and an absolute ban on youth exile as punishment. Armed forces from any faction required civilian authorization for operations beyond immediate defense.

Signatures came in ink, carved marks, and biometric seals. No one called it perfect. Everyone called it necessary.

After the ceremony, Dex leaned toward Jonah and muttered that civilization was just organized arguing with better recordkeeping. Jonah laughed despite himself and replied that recordkeeping was exactly what kept arguments from becoming massacres.

That night, lanterns were lit along ridge paths as a signal to distant settlements: talks had ended without blood. In a world shaped by scarcity and memory, that alone felt radical.
