# Page 5

Routine settled into hard edges over the next six days. Morning crews filtered water and repaired shelters. Midday teams hunted, foraged, and mapped safe routes. Evening councils handled disputes before resentment hardened overnight. Iris built a crude infirmary from cargo tarps and insulation foam. Jonah trained volunteer watch units with whistle codes and fallback points. Dex returned from scouts with useful finds and reckless stories, usually both.

Trust cracked when Dex was discovered carrying a River Clan medallion carved from polished stone. He swore he had found it near the stream. Talan, arriving minutes later with two hunters, said the medallion marked a burial cache and should never have been moved. The accusation ignited shouting across camp lines. Rhea forced a public apology and returned the medallion herself, but the damage lingered.

That night the sky flashed white, brighter than lightning, and a burning object tore across cloud cover before crashing beyond the western ridge. Every person in camp froze, then ran for elevated viewpoints. The signature was unmistakable: Helios hardware.

By dawn, scouts confirmed a secondary shuttle had impacted three kilometers away. If it carried medicine and tools, it could save them through winter rains. If it carried security troops, it could reimpose the same system that had exiled them. At council, voices split between immediate retrieval and defensive caution. Rhea chose a small mixed team to investigate: herself, Jonah, Dex, Iris, and six others. As they left camp, Talan sent one River hunter as observer, neither ally nor enemy, just witness.
