# Page 6

The crashed shuttle lay in a crater of steaming ferns, hull peeled open along one side. Inside they found sealed crates of antibiotics, solar cloth, filtration membranes, and a locked command case stamped with Helios Council insignia. Iris bypassed the seal and projected the message onto a cracked bulkhead.

Directive: establish secure colony zone. Build beacon towers. Detain unstable juvenile assets pending command transfer.

Attached was a custody list.

Rhea’s name appeared third. Dex’s appeared seventh. Twenty-three others were marked for “behavioral containment.”

Before anyone could process it, movement rippled along the crater rim. Ash Wolves emerged in rusted respirators and stitched armor, weapons scavenged from old world ruins. Their leader, Krell, descended slowly and offered terms with unsettling calm: surrender medicine crates and command hardware, and his fighters would grant safe passage.

Jonah refused. A Wolf archer loosed first.

The firefight was short, loud, and chaotic in dense smoke from smoldering brush. Rhea dragged two crates toward cover while Dex and Lenn, a quiet boy from camp, held a flank with station pistols. Iris secured the command case and burned the custody printout rather than let it become leverage. They escaped with half the supplies and two wounded.

Back at camp, the loss hurt, but the discovery hurt more. Helios had not sent support to free them; it had sent structure designed to divide and recage them. Council arguments lasted past midnight. By consensus, they made one decision immediate and unanimous: no detentions without public vote, no secret lists, no hidden authorities.
