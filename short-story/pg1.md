# Page 1

Orbit Station Helios did not collapse all at once; it thinned, like breath on cold glass. Oxygen margins slid downward each week, hydroponic yields failed in uneven waves, and ration alarms became the station’s morning bell. Commander Mira Voss spent her nights signing emergency orders and her days pretending there were still good options. There were not. The Council finally approved Project Descent: one hundred convicted youths sent to Earth to test survivability before the rest of Helios committed to migration. Publicly, it was framed as redemption. Privately, everyone knew it was triage.

Rhea Kane stood at Bay Seven behind reinforced glass while crews sealed the drop ship. She had stolen nutrient injectors for her younger brother and earned a sentence for it. Dex Mercer joked too loudly to hide fear. Jonah Hale stared at mission briefings like they were prayers. Iris Quill held a diagnostic slate, already recording everything, because data felt safer than hope. As launch clamps released, the deck trembled. Below the station, Earth turned in blue silence, unreadable after centuries of myth and warning. Rhea pressed her palm to the window and made a promise she did not say out loud: if the ground was death, she would die useful; if it was life, no one would cage them there again.
