# Page 10

The west ruins rose from mist like drowned bones of a pre-fall city. Broken transit arches cut across flooded plazas where trees grew through concrete and old signage hung in rusted strips. Lenn guided them beneath a collapsed platform to a sealed maintenance vault. Inside: fuel drums, battery stacks, and an intact bunker door marked with military insignia from before orbital evacuation.

Iris bypassed the lock using maintenance keys still embedded in shuttle firmware. The bunker lights flickered on, revealing server racks, weather maps, and archived shelter coordinates spanning half the continent. Humanity had hidden contingencies everywhere, then lost the memory during centuries in orbit.

Copy operations began immediately. Jonah posted perimeter guards. Rhea scanned shelter files for nearby entries. Dex discovered a tactical map showing drone flight corridors that were still compatible with Helios hardware.

The ambush came from above. Wolves dropped chains through skylight fractures while gunfire snapped from upper catwalks. Smoke grenades turned aisles into blind panic. Lenn took a blow shielding Iris and proved his loyalty in blood. Dex held the stairwell despite a deep cut across his shoulder. Rhea triggered emergency shutters, buying thirty seconds to yank data drives free.

They escaped through a drainage tunnel carrying three drives, six battery packs, and one hard lesson: whoever controlled old infrastructure controlled the narrative of survival. Back at camp, the drives were treated like relics. If decoded, they could reveal safe havens, weather windows, and maybe a future larger than one defended valley.
