# Page 13

The climb to Summit Spire began under low cloud and knife-cold wind. Trails narrowed from forest paths to red stone ledges where one misstep meant a long fall into fog. Talan navigated ancient switchbacks marked by carved symbols older than any Helios archive. Along the way they found shrines built from circuit boards, antlers, and polished glass, proof that ground cultures had fused technology and ritual into something wholly new.

By afternoon they sensed pursuit. Shadows tracked ridge lines at distance, disciplined and patient. Wolves, but not striking. Talan read the pattern first: funnel movement. They were being guided toward Needle Gate, a canyon choke point with no easy retreat.

Krell waited there with thirty fighters. He requested parley rather than attack, claiming Vale had promised his tribe medicine, territory guarantees, and legal authority if they kept the sky-born divided. Rhea answered by playing Vale’s transmission through a portable speaker so every fighter in the pass could hear the contempt in his voice.

Krell’s expression shifted from confidence to calculation. Before he could respond, a rifle shot cracked from upper cliffs and killed one of his captains instantly. Additional snipers opened fire on both groups.

Jonah shouted for cover. Dust exploded from basalt walls. In the confusion, Talan led a flanking sprint up a narrow goat path and reached the sniper nest first. Their uniforms bore Helios security markings.

The revelation changed everything. Krell turned his fighters against the snipers, roaring orders through smoke. For twenty brutal minutes, enemies fought shoulder to shoulder because betrayal had clarified alliances faster than diplomacy ever could.
