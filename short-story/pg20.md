# Page 20

One year after descent, the hundred gathered on the ridge above the original wreck site. The drop ship’s rusted plates had been recut into a memorial wall etched with every confirmed name lost, including former enemies. Children born that year chased fireflies through tall grass while elders lit lanterns visible from valley farms, river docks, and mountain watchposts.

Rhea stood beside Dex, Jonah, Iris, Talan, Krell, Lenn, Bo Lin, and Sera as Commander Mira Voss appeared by radio link from Helios archive orbit. The station no longer ruled; it relayed data, preserved records, and supplied what ground councils requested under Accord rules. Power had moved from command to consent, unevenly but undeniably.

At sunset, Rhea read the founding article added after the first winter: No person shall be abandoned to preserve another’s comfort. Emergency burden must be shared, publicly accounted, and reviewable by those who bear it. The line had been debated for days before adoption. Now it was recited from memory by people who once would have killed each other over medicine crates.

Storms still came. Harvests still failed sometimes. Old resentments resurfaced in lean months. None of that vanished. What changed was the response. They argued in councils before drawing blades. They published shortages before panic began. They treated wounded without checking faction marks first.

As darkness settled, the lantern chain brightened across the horizon like a stitched seam holding broken land together. The world remained unfinished, dangerous, and worth the work.
