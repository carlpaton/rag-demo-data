First contact came with fog still clinging to the ground. Four figures stepped from cedar shadows wearing layered bark armor, bone pendants, and woven cloaks darkened by rain. Their bows were unstrung but held in open hands. The woman in front introduced herself in rough Station tongue as Talan, speaker for the River Clan. She studied the crash wreckage without awe, as if she had watched falling metal all her life.

Talan asked one question: “Guests or takers?”

Dex answered first with swagger and promises of trade. Jonah answered with caution, listing what they needed to survive. Rhea answered by placing a sealed ration packet and a canteen on a stump between them, then stepping back. Talan did not touch either. She pointed to the camp’s cut saplings and said the river had boundaries even in crisis.

Negotiations stumbled through shared words and gestures. Iris drew maps in mud while Talan corrected river names and warned of poisoned flats to the east. Before leaving, she gave a final warning about the Ash Wolves, raiders from burned plains who took medicine and hostages in the same raids.

As the clan delegation disappeared into mist, Dex muttered they looked outnumbered and vulnerable. Jonah replied that confident people rarely advertise their numbers. By afternoon the camp agreed to a temporary nonaggression signal protocol: white flare for trade, red flare for threat. It was a fragile beginning, but it transformed the valley from unknown wilderness into political territory. Survival would require not only food and shelter, but diplomacy, restraint, and the humility to learn from people they had been taught to fear.
