The day after the crater attack, panic moved through camp like a second weather system. Every whispered rumor became strategy advice: Wolves were massing by the ridge, River Clan had withdrawn support, Helios command ships were already en route. Rhea shut down speculation and called full assembly at noon.

She read the shuttle directive and custody list aloud in front of everyone, including the names marked for detention. Anger shifted from abstract fear to clear target. Dex argued for burning every Helios order and declaring total independence immediately. Jonah warned that rejecting all structure would collapse them faster than any enemy. Iris offered a third path: write a temporary charter with limited powers, rotating leadership, and mandatory review every ten days.

Debate was brutal but productive. The final charter established open inventories, elected work captains, mixed tribunal panels for disputes, and a strict ban on youth confinement without supermajority vote. It passed by six voices.

Talan attended the assembly silently, then surprised everyone by asking to copy the charter symbols onto bark sheets for her council. “Words can be traps,” she said, “but they can also be fences against worse traps.”

That evening, while new duty rosters were assigned, an east perimeter alarm sounded. Wolves had approached, cut one wire line, and retreated without casualties. On a carved post they left a warning: SKY-BORN KNEEL OR BURN.

Jonah ordered double watches and distributed flare codes to every tent block. No one complained this time. Governance had moved from theory to necessity, and each person now understood the same truth: their biggest vulnerability was not lack of weapons, but lack of unity under pressure.
