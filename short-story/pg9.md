# Page 9

A three-day storm pinned everyone to shelter and stripped morale to essentials. Trails turned to black mud, firewood soaked through, and trap lines washed out downstream. Food projections dropped below safe margin by forty-eight hours. Under pressure, old instincts resurfaced: hoarding, accusations, and loyalty to friends over system.

During the second night, guards dragged in a wounded teenager marked with Ash Wolf tattoos. He gave his name as Lenn and claimed he had fled after refusing Krell’s order to execute captives. Jonah demanded restraints until verification. Rhea demanded treatment first, questioning second. The argument exploded at triage in front of half the camp.

Iris ended it by setting Lenn’s fractured arm herself and assigning supervised watch duty. “Mercy with accountability,” she said, writing it on the board.

At dawn Lenn offered intelligence: a fuel and battery cache hidden in west ruins under old transit platforms. Enough power, he claimed, to run perimeter flares and sensor beacons for months. It sounded like exactly what they needed and exactly the kind of bait Wolves would use.

The council voted 11-8 to investigate with a limited strike-retrieval team. Talan advised using river fog for approach concealment. Dex volunteered first despite lingering shoulder pain. Jonah accepted Lenn as guide but paired him with two guards and explicit fail-safes.

As they departed, rain finally eased. The valley steamed under weak sunlight, beautiful and indifferent. Rhea walked point with map slate in one hand and rifle in the other, repeating the same equation in her head: trust too little and they die isolated; trust too much and they die betrayed.
